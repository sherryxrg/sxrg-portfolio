Prototypes for hackathons

I started attending hackathons in fall of last year, and whether it be learning a new language in 24 hours or learning how to resolve conflicts in a team, I have never learned so much in the matter of a few intense days.

TheirCard (2020)

Role: Backend, Frontend, UI/UX

Tools: XCode 10 + Swift 4

Inspired by sites like Elpha, we created a virtual buisness-card app that lets users connect and learn about what its like being a women in their work environment. Because of the 24hr time constraint, we chose to make an iOS app, which simplified data storage and the design process, so that we could focus on our ideas.

DriveWell(2019)

Role: Backend, Frontend

Tools: XCode 10 + Swift 4 (CoreMotion Library for Accelerometer)

The experience I'm most proud of is at Decongestion Hacks, where we installed XCode, learned Swift, and built a working app using Apple's CoreMotion library in 48 hours. This was also where I got to know most of my team members for the LunarFest iOS app project.

SafeBike(2019)

Role: Frontend

Tools: HTML5, CSS+SASS, JS/Jquery

Drawing on my previous experience as a first responder, our team created an app that lets bike-race attendees with first aid personnel. I got to practice using a lot of SASS to refactor my CSS code here.
